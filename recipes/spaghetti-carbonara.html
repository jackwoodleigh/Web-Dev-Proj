Spaghetti Carbonara

'

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta or guanciale (diced)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g pecorino cheese (grated)
  • 50g parmesan cheese (grated)
  • 2 cloves garlic (peeled and crushed)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat a small amount of olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the pancetta (or guanciale) and garlic. Cook until the meat becomes crispy and golden.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated pecorino, and parmesan cheeses, then season with a pinch of black pepper.
  4. Once the pasta is cooked, reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water and drain the rest. Remove the garlic from the pan and discard it.
  5. Quickly toss the hot pasta in the pan with the pancetta to coat it in the rendered fat.
  6. Remove the pan from the heat and immediately pour in the egg and cheese mixture, tossing quickly to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time until the desired consistency is achieved.
  7. Serve the carbonara in warm bowls, and garnish with extra grated cheese and black pepper. Enjoy!

Tips

  • For a creamier texture, you can add a splash of cream, though it's not traditional in classic carbonara recipes.
  • Make sure the pasta is hot when mixing with the egg mixture to create a smooth sauce.
